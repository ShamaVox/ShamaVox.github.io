<section class="section" id="why">

  <div class="wrap">

    <p class="eyebrow">Why teams call me</p>

    <h2>Every AI project I have watched fail, failed the same way.</h2>

    <p class="lede rv">

      The demo worked. Everyone was happy. Then real data showed up, or a real customer did,

      and the thing that looked finished turned out to be about 40% of the work.

    </p>

    <p class="lede rv">

      The missing 60% is almost always the same. Nothing checks whether the model was right.

      The model decides when it is done. Retries run until the bill arrives. And when something

      breaks, nobody can reconstruct what happened.

    </p>

    <p class="lede rv">

      I have built that layer four times. I would rather help you build it before the rewrite than after.

    </p>

        <h3>Audit</h3>

<section class="section tight" id="work">

  <div class="wrap">

    <p class="eyebrow">Selected work</p>

    <h2>Same pattern in all of them.</h2>

    <p class="lede rv">

      Something generates, something else checks it, and there is a defined path for when the check

      fails. That is what separates a system a company will run from one that only demos well.

    </p>


    <div class="cases" style="margin-top:34px">


      <article class="case rv">

        <div>

          <span class="org">AI-native retail platform</span>

          <h3>Multi-agent document intelligence</h3>

        </div>

        <dl>

          <dt>Context</dt>

          <dd>Retailers were losing thousands of hours to manual data entry from vendor documents. Usually right is not a standard you can run a business on.</dd>

          <dt>Architecture</dt>

          <dd>Multi-agent extraction turning messy vendor documents into typed, validated records. <strong>Confidence scoring</strong> on every field, <strong>bounded retry</strong> instead of open-ended loops, and escalation to a human when the score drops. When it fails it fails into a known state rather than a plausible wrong one.</dd>

          <dt>Result</dt>

          <dd class="result"><strong>96% extraction accuracy</strong> in production with live paying merchants. Built a custom onboarding path around a platform certification bottleneck so sales did not stall.</dd>

          <dt>Stack</dt>

          <dd>Claude API, multi-agent orchestration, confidence scoring, human review tooling, commerce platform integration.</dd>

        </dl>

      </article>


      <article class="case rv">

        <div>

          <span class="org">Nickelytics</span>

          <h3>Edge AI telemetry for autonomous assets</h3>

        </div>

        <dl>

          <dt>Context</dt>

          <dd>Turning moving vehicles into measurable advertising inventory. That needs real attribution, not an estimate someone can argue with.</dd>

          <dt>Architecture</dt>

          <dd>Built real-time location tracking, <strong>edge attribution telemetry</strong>, and computer vision running on the vehicle. Wrote the software foundations from nothing and built the team to run them.</dd>

          <dt>Result</dt>

          <dd class="result">Scaled recurring revenue from <strong>$20K MRR to $3.5M ARR</strong>, owning product, delivery, infrastructure cost, and the org through the whole climb.</dd>

          <dt>Stack</dt>

          <dd>Edge computer vision, real-time geospatial tracking, attribution pipeline, cloud infrastructure engineered for sublinear cost scaling.</dd>

        </dl>

      </article>


      <article class="case rv">

        <div>

          <span class="org">SignAI</span>

          <h3>Real-time multimodal translation</h3>

        </div>

        <dl>

          <dt>Context</dt>

          <dd>Sign language interpretation is a latency problem dressed up as a translation problem. An interpreter that lags is not an interpreter.</dd>

          <dt>Architecture</dt>

          <dd>Bidirectional real-time ASL and English interpretation using gesture and pose modeling, low-latency inference, and edge optimization on commodity hardware.</dd>

          <dt>Result</dt>

          <dd class="result">Delivered a <strong>working real-time bidirectional prototype</strong> on commodity hardware and ran technical validation demos for <strong>Microsoft</strong>. Handed the platform off as a Deaf-led initiative. Accessibility work belongs with the community it serves.</dd>

          <dt>Stack</dt>

          <dd>Computer vision, gesture and pose modeling, low-latency inference, bidirectional translation pipeline.</dd>

        </dl>

      </article>


      <article class="case rv">

        <div>

          <span class="org">BrainEx &middot; Side project</span>

          <h3>Confidential support for students in India</h3>

        </div>

        <dl>

          <dt>Context</dt>

          <dd>Students in India face significant mental health pressure and very little confidential support. The barrier is rarely willingness to seek help. It is the fear that seeking it will be visible to family, faculty, or peers.</dd>

          <dt>Architecture</dt>

          <dd>A privacy-first data architecture where confidentiality is a system property rather than a policy document. Strict access controls, data minimisation, and audit logging on every sensitive read.</dd>

          <dt>Result</dt>

          <dd class="result">A support workflow students could actually use, with the privacy guarantees <strong>enforced in the architecture</strong> rather than promised on a terms page.</dd>

        </dl>

      </article>
